## 1. Who we sign

**Target (launch — Abuja):** Maitama, Wuse II, Asokoro, Garki, Gwarinpa. Restaurants, lounges, and bars that take **bookings** and feel **no‑show pain** — mid‑to‑upscale venues with a reservation culture and a diplomatic/government/expat clientele.

**Best‑fit signals:** takes phone/Instagram bookings today, complains about no‑shows, preps ahead, has a host/manager who owns the floor.

**Why Abuja first:** higher real spending power, diners who book ahead and actually arrive, tight restaurant clusters (easy founder‑led acquisition), no dominant local competitor. We prove the model here before anywhere else.

**Goal:** 30–80 quality venues, founder‑led, using existing industry relationships.

## 2. The 60‑second pitch

> “You know the tables you lose to no‑shows every week? Bucr fixes that. When someone books you on Bucr, they put down a small **refundable deposit**. If they show up, they get it back **plus a bonus** — if they don’t, **you get paid** for the table you held. It’s **free to list**, you only pay a small flat fee **when a guest actually shows up and sits down**, and we send you diners who are serious. No commission on your food, no monthly fee to start. Can I set you up in 15 minutes?”

Three things to land: **(1) kills no‑shows, (2) free to start / pay‑per‑result, (3) no commission on the bill.**

---

## 3. The full pitch

Walk the owner through the loop:

1. **The problem.** “Half of Lagos bookings no‑show. You prep, you hold the table, they don’t come, you lose the cover and the walk‑in.”
2. **The fix — a deposit that means something.** “On Bucr the guest pays a **small refundable credit deposit** to confirm. That filters out the casual bookers.”
3. **Guest shows up** → “They get their deposit back **+3%**. You just pay us a **flat fee per seated guest** — ₦1,500 on the free plan, less or nothing on paid plans. Never a % of the bill.”
4. **Guest no‑shows** → “They lose 40% of the deposit. **You get 30% of it** as credit — compensation for the held table. We keep a little. You’re no longer losing for free.”
5. **You keep the bill.** “Guests pay you for the food **at the venue, like always.** We never touch your money — we only handle the deposit.”
6. **You get discovery.** “You’re listed to diners across the Bucr app and website, with reviews, photos, and a verified badge.”

**The one‑liner to repeat:** *“Free to list. Pay only when they show. Get paid when they don’t.”*

## 4. Live demo script

Have the **diner app/website** and a **demo vendor portal** open.

**On the diner side (2 min):**
1. Search a restaurant → open a venue page → show photos, rating, “Book with Confidence” badge.
2. Tap **Reserve** → pick date/time/party → show the **refundable deposit** line (“they get this back +3%”).
3. Confirm → show the **QR + PIN** the guest brings.

**On the vendor side (3 min):**
1. **Dashboard** → “here’s today’s covers.”
2. **QR Scanner** → scan the demo QR → “check‑in takes two seconds; this releases their deposit and records the cover.”
3. **Reservations** → show statuses; show marking a **no‑show** → “this is where you get compensated.”
4. **Settings → deposit / hours**, **Menu**, **Featured Spots** (mention ads later, don’t oversell on day one).

Keep it under 6 minutes. Let them hold the phone.

## 5. Sign‑up: onboard in 15 minutes

Do this **with** the owner, on their device where possible (so they own the login).

1. Go to the vendor portal → **Register your restaurant**.
2. **Owner details** — country (Nigeria), owner name, email, phone, password. *(Confirm they can access that email — verification + receipts go there.)*
3. **Business info** — type, **venue type** (this sets their default deposit: Fine Dining ₦20k, Upscale ₦15k, Lounge/Casual ₦10k), name, 1–2 line description, cuisines.
4. **Location** — address + **drop the map pin on the actual front door** (critical for directions).
5. **Documents** — photograph the **CAC certificate** and **owner’s ID** and upload.
6. Submit → status is **Pending**. Tell them: “you’ll be approved within [X]; meanwhile let’s set up your page.”

While pending, set up the page so it’s launch‑ready (see next section).

## 6. Train the vendor — the essential 5

Don’t teach everything. Teach the **five things that make it work**, then hand them the [Vendor Guide](./VENDOR_GUIDE.md).

1. **Check guests in — every time.** “Open **QR Scanner**, scan or type the PIN. If you don’t check them in, the guest’s deposit doesn’t release and you don’t get credit for the cover. This is the one habit that matters.”
2. **Mark genuine no‑shows.** “If they truly don’t come, mark **No‑show** — that’s how you get compensated. Be fair; only real no‑shows.”
3. **Set the deposit & hours.** Settings → confirm the deposit feels right for their venue; set opening hours (powers “Open now”).
4. **Make the page sell.** Upload **great photos** (cover image first), finish the description, add the **menu** with prices.
5. **Respond to reviews.** “A calm reply to a bad review wins more bookings than ten good ones.”

Then mention the growth levers (don’t hard‑sell day one): **Pro** (analytics, CRM, offers, 50% off cover fees), **Elite** (experiences, featured ads, zero cover fees), and the **Book‑with‑Confidence badge**.

## 7. Objection handling

| They say | You say |
|---|---|
| **“What does it cost?”** | “Free to list. You only pay a flat **₦1,500 per guest who actually shows up** — and that drops to ₦750 or ₦0 on paid plans. Never a cut of your food bill.” |
| **“My guests won’t pay a deposit.”** | “It’s small, **fully refundable**, and they get a **3% bonus** for showing. Serious diners love it — it guarantees their table. It only filters out the people who were going to ghost you anyway.” |
| **“I already use Instagram/phone bookings.”** | “Keep those. Bucr just adds the bookings that *hold*, plus discovery to new diners — and it does the no‑show math for you automatically.” |
| **“Is this like the apps that take 20–30%?”** | “No. We **never** take a percentage of your bill — that’s the whole point. A flat per‑cover fee, only when they show.” |
| **“Can I take my deposit money out as cash?”** | “Vendor credits are **marketing credits** for visibility on Bucr — they’re not cash. Your **food revenue is 100% yours**, paid at the venue. This keeps the platform simple and above‑board.” |
| **“I’m too busy to manage another app.”** | “The only daily action is **scan to check in** — two seconds per guest. We’ll set everything else up with you now.” |
| **“What if a guest says they came but didn’t?”** | “Check‑in is on your side — only a real scan/PIN counts. Be fair and you’ll never have disputes.” |

## 8. First‑week success plan

- **Day 0 (sign‑up):** complete profile, photos, menu, hours, deposit. Confirm they can log in on the device they’ll use at the host stand.
- **Day 1–2:** confirm approval; do a **test booking** end‑to‑end (you book on the diner app, they check you in). This builds confidence fast.
- **Day 3–5:** check they’re checking guests in. Most early failures are “forgot to scan.” Reinforce habit #1.
- **Day 7:** review their first numbers together (covers, any no‑shows compensated). Celebrate the first “you got paid for a no‑show” moment — it’s the aha.
- **Week 2:** if they’re active, introduce **Pro** (or **Elite** for ambitious venues) and one **Special Offer** to fill a slow night.

---

## 9. Do’s & don’ts

**Do**
- Set them up live; leave them with a working login and the Vendor Guide.
- Be precise on money: **flat per‑cover, never a % of the bill; food paid at venue.**
- Emphasise check‑in as the one essential habit.

**Don’t (compliance guardrails — important)**
- **Never** promise vendors can **cash out** credits. They cannot. Credits are marketing‑only.
- **Never** say Bucr “holds” or “pays out” their meal revenue. We don’t — money flows only *to* Bucr (fees, subscriptions, ad credits).
- **Never** quote a % of the bill as the fee. It’s always a flat per‑cover.
- Don’t over‑promise featured/ads ROI — show the **Views/Clicks/CTR** metrics and let the numbers speak.
- Don’t invent economic figures — the canonical numbers are below and in `CLAUDE.md`. Treat pricing as “validate with the vendor,” not gospel, where noted.

## 11. Glossary & quick numbers

| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Credit** | Bucr’s unit. **1 credit = ₦10.** |
| **Deposit** | Flat refundable credits to confirm a booking. Default by venue type (Fine ₦20k / Upscale ₦15k / Lounge·Casual ₦10k); vendor can override. |
| **Show‑up bonus** | Guest gets deposit back **+3%** on check‑in. |
| **No‑show split** | Guest forfeits **40%** → **30% to vendor** (credits) + **10% to Bucr**. |
| **Per‑cover fee** | Flat fee per seated guest: **₦1,500 Basic · ₦750 Pro · ₦0 Elite.** Never a % of the bill. |
| **Cancellation** | 24h+ 100% · 12–24h 50% · <12h 0% · vendor‑cancel 100% + 10% bonus to guest. |
| **Credit expiry** | 90 days (reminder 30 days before). |
| **Subscriptions** | Basic free · **Pro ₦30k/mo** · **Elite ₦85k/mo**. |
| **Featured (ads)** | Elite‑only access; buy placements with credits (Spotlight/Prime/Headline…). |
| **Book‑with‑Confidence** | Trust badge for vendors at 95%+ reliability sustained 60 days. |

> ⚠️ Pricing figures (esp. per‑cover ₦1,500 and the 6% credit spread) are reasoned estimates pending validation with 15–20 Abuja vendors. If an owner pushes back hard on a number, **note it and report it** — that feedback is gold.
