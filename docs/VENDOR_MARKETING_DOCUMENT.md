# BUCR Vendor Partnership Guide

## A practical business case for restaurants, bars, and lounges

---

## 1) What BUCR is

BUCR is a reservation and customer growth platform built for hospitality businesses in Nigeria. It helps venues reduce no-shows, improve table utilization, and increase repeat customer visits through a credit-backed booking model.

Unlike ad-heavy marketplaces that only drive traffic, BUCR focuses on **attendance quality** and **customer commitment**:

- Guests place a credit deposit to secure bookings.
- Guests who show up get their deposit back (plus bonus credits).
- Guests who no-show forfeit their deposit.

This model is designed to align guest behavior with vendor revenue goals.

---

## 2) The problem BUCR solves for vendors

Most restaurants, bars, and lounges face the same revenue leaks:

- Frequent no-shows and late cancellations.
- Unpredictable covers that disrupt staffing and prep planning.
- Empty tables during prime hours.
- Weak customer retention from one-time visits.
- Limited visibility into guest behavior and preferences.

No-shows are not just an inconvenience; they are lost revenue from food prep waste, idle staff time, and missed high-value walk-ins.

---

## 3) How BUCR solves it

### Credit-backed reservations (anti-no-show engine)

BUCR uses credit deposits by party size:

- 1-2 guests: 50 credits
- 3-6 guests: 100 credits
- 7+ guests: 200 credits

Guests check in with QR or PIN at the venue. This gives vendors a fast and verifiable arrival process while reinforcing booking accountability.

### Behavioral incentives that favor attendance

- **Guest shows up:** deposit is refunded + bonus credit awarded.
- **Guest cancels early:** full or partial refund based on policy window.
- **Guest no-shows/late cancels:** deposit is forfeited.

Result: stronger attendance intent and fewer speculative bookings.

### Vendor operations in one place

BUCR gives venues tools to:

- Manage reservations and check-ins in real time (QR / PIN).
- Handle no-show marking and booking states clearly.
- Manage menus, gallery, and profile presentation.
- Track guest profiles and preferences for better hospitality.
- Manage reviews and customer feedback responses.
- Run a **waitlist** — when you're full, guests join and you notify the next party when a
  table frees (recover would-be-lost covers).
- See **transparent billing** — the per-cover fee is invoiced every two weeks and paid by
  card; you only ever pay for guests who actually showed up.
- Manage **multiple branches** (each with its own hours and map pin).
- Get all alerts + announcements in one **Message Center** (also emailed).

## 4) Vendor benefits (business impact)

## A) Higher realized sales from better attendance

When more booked guests actually show up, you capture more expected revenue per service period. The platform is designed to protect prime-time demand from disappearing at the last minute.

## B) Better table utilization and service planning

With more reliable reservations, managers can:

- Plan staffing with higher confidence.
- Reduce over-prep and wastage.
- Allocate high-value time slots more efficiently.

## C) Improved guest quality and loyalty loop

Guests who book through BUCR are not casual clickers; they commit credits and are motivated to honor reservations. The platform creates a repeat cycle where loyal guests keep returning to use credits and bonuses.

## D) More control than traditional commission marketplaces

BUCR is designed around vendor partnership and predictable operating value, with subscription-led monetization rather than pure transaction extraction on every order.

## E) Better brand presence and discovery

Your venue profile, menu, gallery, and experiences are discoverable in the BUCR ecosystem, helping you attract new guests while keeping operational control.

## F) Proof of value you can see ("Your BUCR impact")

Your dashboard shows, for the last 30 days: your **show-up rate**, the **deposit value
protected** (your guests' money at stake to actually turn up), the **no-show compensation**
you earned, and **covers seated**. You don't have to take "fewer no-shows" on faith — the
numbers are in front of you, which makes the per-cover fee easy to justify.

## 5) Why this can increase sales for your venue

BUCR drives sales through four practical levers:

1. **No-show reduction:** more of your reserved guests convert to seated customers.
2. **Prime-hour protection:** fewer last-minute gaps during highest revenue windows.
3. **Repeat visits:** credit and bonus mechanics encourage return behavior.
4. **Operational efficiency:** better demand visibility improves staff and inventory decisions.

Even without changing your menu, these levers can increase realized daily turnover by improving conversion of existing demand.

## 8) Common objections and strong responses

### "Will this scare guests away?"

Serious guests adapt quickly when a booking system is fair and transparent. BUCR rewards attendance with refunds/bonuses, which makes the model feel positive, not punitive.

### "Will staff find it hard to use?"

Core operations are straightforward: booking list, QR/PIN check-in, and status updates. The system is designed for day-to-day service flow, not technical complexity.

### "How does this help revenue in practice?"

Revenue improves when reserved seats convert into seated guests more consistently. BUCR directly targets that conversion gap.

### "Is this only for fine dining?"

No. The model is useful for restaurants, bars, lounges, and experience-led venues where attendance reliability affects profitability.
